# Synthesis Patterns

Structures for combining findings into conclusions.

---

## Argument Structure

**Thesis-led:**
1. State conclusion first
2. Present supporting evidence
3. Address counter-arguments
4. Reaffirm with nuance

**Evidence-led:**
1. Present findings by theme
2. Show how they build toward conclusion
3. Acknowledge limitations
4. State conclusion with confidence level

---

## Handling Contradictions

**Reconcile:** Find the variable that explains the difference
**Contextualize:** Explain when each finding applies
**Prioritize:** Weight by evidence strength
**Acknowledge:** State that contradiction exists without resolution

---

## Confidence Levels

Assign to each conclusion:
- **High:** Multiple strong sources agree
- **Medium:** Some evidence supports, gaps exist
- **Low:** Limited evidence, further research needed
- **Inconclusive:** Evidence is contradictory or insufficient

---

## Gap Acknowledgment

Explicitly state:
- What questions remain unanswered
- What evidence was unavailable
- What methodology limitations exist
- What assumptions were made

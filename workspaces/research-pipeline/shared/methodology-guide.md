# Methodology Guide

Common research methodologies and when to use each.

---

## Literature Review

**Use when:** Synthesizing existing knowledge on a topic
**Produces:** Summary of current state, gaps, debates
**Process:** Systematic search, screening, extraction, synthesis

---

## Case Study

**Use when:** Understanding a specific instance in depth
**Produces:** Detailed analysis of one case with transferable insights
**Process:** Select case, gather data, analyze patterns, draw conclusions

---

## Comparative Analysis

**Use when:** Understanding differences and similarities across options
**Produces:** Side-by-side comparison with criteria and findings
**Process:** Define criteria, gather comparable data, analyze differences

---

## Exploratory Research

**Use when:** Topic is new or poorly understood
**Produces:** Initial map of the territory, questions for further research
**Process:** Broad search, identify themes, refine focus

---

## Evidence Synthesis

**Use when:** Combining findings from multiple studies
**Produces:** Weighted conclusions based on evidence strength
**Process:** Assess evidence quality, aggregate findings, judge confidence

---

## Gap Analysis

**Use when:** Identifying what is missing in current knowledge
**Produces:** List of gaps with priorities for future research
**Process:** Map existing knowledge, identify missing areas, prioritize

---

## Selecting a Methodology

1. Start with your research question type
2. Consider what output would answer it best
3. Match methodology to available resources
4. Ensure methodology can produce defensible conclusions
